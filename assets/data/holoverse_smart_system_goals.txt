HoloVerse Smart System Goals — Pass 22
======================================

Purpose
-------
Turn the site HoloVerse demo from a passive miniature world into a readable smart ecosystem. Entities should take form as small colored bots, move through region-specific jobs, and make the world feel alive without adding heavy rendering cost.

Entity Form
-----------
- Entities should appear as tiny bots, not plain dots.
- Bots should use different colors and silhouettes based on their home region.
- Preferred style: lightweight mini-wireframe / pseudo-3D canvas shapes so the demo looks dimensional without hurting browser performance.
- Bots can carry small glowing resource markers when harvesting, transporting, building, or constructing.

Region Purposes
---------------
- Flat: central hub for gatherings, events, handoffs, and world coordination.
- Forests: flora harvesting zone.
- Green Hills: fauna tending / animal-resource zone.
- Mushroom: vegetation, spores, and wetland bio-material growth zone.
- Ice: fast road/freeway ring for faster travel between sides of the regions.
- Desert: slower traffic lane for heavy cargo, caravans, and archive routes.
- Urban: bots build their own unique neighborhoods, aligned toward the center of mass.
- Metropolis: peak-success shell; strong bots rise here, weaker bots are sent back to Urban.
- Space: exterior Dyson sphere construction layer, visually resembling the red wireframe Dyson shell from the full HoloVerse direction.

Smart Behavior Goals
--------------------
- Bots should harvest resources from the regions that produce what they need.
- Movement should communicate intent: harvesting, tending, growing, routing, building, sorting, or constructing.
- Ice traffic should visibly move faster than Desert traffic.
- Urban structures should form readable neighborhood clusters aligned around the world center.
- Metropolis should act like a success/sorting ring instead of just another city band.
- Space should show ongoing Dyson construction progress as an exterior wireframe shell.

Performance Rules
-----------------
- Keep the site demo lightweight.
- Prefer procedural canvas graphics over image-heavy assets.
- Reuse simple geometry patterns: rings, small cubes, diamonds, circles, hex bots, pyramids, trees, and thin wireframe structures.
- Avoid first-person/cockpit/FPS systems for the website version.
- Keep the 16:9 top-down ring-world readability and existing lightweight controls.
